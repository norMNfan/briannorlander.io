Vietnam, Fall 2018

Visiting Hanoi, and Sapa from September 26 to October 02, 2018.

Just 12 after after coming back from China, I boarded a plane bound for Hanoi with Christian, a fellow exchange student I met at HKUST. We would were in Vietnam for 6 days during a long weekend starting in the bustling capital of Hanoi and then exploring the mountains and valleys surrounding Sapa.

Hanoi

The capital and 2nd largest city in Vietnam, Hanoi is located on the Red River and has been the most important city politically in the region for over 1,000 years. First part of the Nguyen dynasty, Hanoi was conquered by the French in 1873. Since then it has been the administrative center of French Indochina until finally the Vietnamese people won their independence from colonial powers in 1945.

Our ride from the airport to the city center was wild. The driver ducked in and out of traffic on streets that didn't seem to have any speed limits while chuckling when we would react on his driving style.

The exchange rate was 1 USD to 23,500 Vietnamese Dong. After my first ATM withdrawal I was a multi-millionaire.

The Hanoi Hilton was a prison first used by the French to house Vietnamese dissenters and then later used in the Vietnam War to house Americans. John McCain spent time here.

Ho Chi Minh, a key figure in the founding and independence wars of Vietnam, had an elaborate palace in Hanoi. His mausoleum was also near the city center.

And that's a wrap for Hanoi! At the place we stayed in Hanoi we met two other travelers who wanted to go to Sapa as well. We booked bus tickets with them and left bright and early the next morning.

Sapa

Sapa is a frontier township in north-west Vietnam. The infrastructure was built up by the French to serve as an escape from the heat and a resort town. Historically it has been used by the multitude of minority groups in the region as a central market town.

We arrived at our hostel and it was surreal. We had a view that overlooked the city with mist en crowned mountains as a backdrop. In the lobby traditional Vietnamese music was playing and hot tea was served all day. This was the perfect spot to chill out, which is what we did.

The hostel was a capsule hostel, giving it a very futuristic look. I would definitely recommend a capsule hostel even though it looks very odd. You get privacy and quiet.

After spending some time chilling at the hostel and exploring the city we ventured out and checked out some of the nearby valleys and villages.

Muong Hoa Valley

The next morning I took a guided tour with a local lady from a nearby village. When we started in the morning the hills were shrouded in mist. After we ate lunch at a villager community center the sun started to peak through the clouds and we got some amazing views of the Muong Hoa Valley rice terraces.

There are four main minority groups, all with different cultures, customs, garments, and languages.
