Hong Kong, Fall 2018

Visiting Hong Kong from August 29 to January 01, 2019.

In the fall of 2018 I participated in an exchange program in Hong Kong at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.

Nature

Although Hong Kong is known for its skyscrapers, much of the territory is hilly, forested, and undeveloped. This allows for many hiking trails and camping opportunites of which I took advantage of.

The northeastern region of Hong Kong is called Sai Kung. This was close to where my unversity was and also where I did most of my excursions.

Suicide Cliff hike

Typhoon Mangkhut

Two weeks after arriving in Hong Kong a category 5 super typhoon ripped through the city. It was the strongest typhoon to hit the city since 1983.

My dorm room overlooked the ocean, so I had a front row view to the storm. For 24 hours all students were locked down in their dormitories, not allowed to leave. Windows were reinforced with sand bags, benches were tied down and super markets were nearly empty. It was surreal.

Villages

Hong Kong also has many small fishing villages on its coast. The pace of life in these peaceful villages is much slower than the city and is a sharp contrast from the hustle and the cities hustle and bustle.

Temples

Hong Kong has many parks and temples, allowing an escape from a sometimes claustrophobic cityscape.

Food

Hong Kong is known for its noodle bowls and dim sum. However the city boasts many cuisines from different cultures from all over Asia and the West.

At my university I was allowed to put on a Thanksgiving dinner. Some other exchange students helped me out who also was missing the family tradition of Thanksgiving, which occurs every year in late November. We were able to share this tradiational meal with some locals and other international students.
