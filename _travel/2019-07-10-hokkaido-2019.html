During a long weekend in the middle of July I flew to Japan's northern most island, Hokkaido. I rented a car near Sapparo and then drove 1200km in 5 days, circling the island and getting a taste for what this wild land had to offer.

Hokkaido is known for its cold winters, many volcanos, bears, and many of Japans most popular food such as milk, cheese, sushi, ramen, and much more. In Japan, Hokkaido is popular for its nature and food so naturally this was the focus of the trip.

Day 1

After flying from Tokyo to Sappro on Day 0, we technically started Day 1 around midnight when we arrived at the New Chitose Airport.

Originally we had planned on sleeping at the airport, but it soon became obvious that sleeping overnight at the airport was not allowed. Luckily, there was an onsen at the airport! Japan is awesome. We entered the onsen just before midnight and took a long and relaxing, gender seperated spa session before retiring to a large, unisex room with reclining chairs to sleep.

The first day we went from New Chitose Airport to Hell Valley to Mt. Yotei to Yoichi.
