During the winter break of my Junior year of college I took a trip to the Philippines and Hong Kong with my uncle, cousin and cousin's boyfriend.

We visited 6 islands in the Philippine archipelago including Luzon, Mindinau, Panay, Negros, Samal, and Corregidor. Additionally we took a flight up to Hong Kong for one day. We saw a lot!

Manila

People, Culture, Chaos

Manila, the capitol of the Philippines, has the most dense city proper in the world and is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on earth.

Our first night was spent in Manila. We were welcomed with humidity, diesel smoke, and crowded streets. This was my first time being in Asia and it was a huge culture shock. Exhausted from a 24 hour flight and 13 hour time difference, the next day we walked around the Baclaran Market and took the MRT in a loop around the city.

Davao City

Tropics, Fruit, Ocean

Davao City is located in the tropical rain forest climate and is home to many amazing fruits and animals. We got to try durian, pomelo, pineapple, papaya, mango, lasones, marang and bananas!

We were picked up at the airport by Precey and Renee (my cousin's aunt and uncle, who aren't related to me).

The next day we took to the streets of Davao in search of the infamous durian. Durian is said to "taste like heaven and smell like hell". Taking this as a challenge we sniffed out the first durian stand we came across.

If you don't wear gloves while eating durian your hands will smell for days. It is also banned in most hotels!

That night family treated us to an authentic Filipino dinner. Chicken Adobo (my favorite), mango smoothies, marang, pomelo, and many other traditional Filipino dishes were served.

Lake Sebu

Lakes, Rain Forest, Fish

Lake Sebu is a remote lake on the western side of Mindanao. The surrounding region is very rural and recently has had some terrorism scares. It is close to Marawi City.
